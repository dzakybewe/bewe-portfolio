A fully offline Point-of-Sale and inventory management app for a small restaurant, complete with sales tracking, menu management, and Excel-based financial reporting.

A restaurant owner needed a cashier app, but the subscription-based options on the market felt too expensive for a small business. They wanted something they owned, so a friend connected us. I built a Point-of-Sale and inventory system from scratch, solo, in one month. The client wasn't concerned with how it looked, he cared about whether it worked. So I focused on function, translating each operational need into a feature. The trickiest part was drag-and-drop menu reordering, something he casually requested without knowing what it takes to build. I figured it out anyway.

Managing a non-technical client taught me that requirements shift, expectations are hard to pin down, and finding middle ground is as much the job as writing code. The satisfaction of handing over something that genuinely solves a problem for someone's livelihood made it worth every scope change.
